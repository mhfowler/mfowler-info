TI-89 & The Poetics Of Space

There is a popular way of understanding the world, as a collection of particles interacting based on physical laws, which leads many to the conclusion that there is no such thing as "free will", something a great many people have got hung up about in textbooks, ethical manifestos, and at diners at 3am trying to make sense of all this stardust.

Perhaps the question of "free will" in the sense of how to find or create the mental fortitude to make the decisions we should is more urgent than the quasi-metaphysical question of what causes those decisions in "Me".

Some dogmatic determinists seem to want to replace any notion of a person with a set of gravitational equations which may have already been solved somewhere in a middle school classroom in rural arkansas by a precocious algebra student doing some extra-curricular work on their TI-89.
