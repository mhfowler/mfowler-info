in the chapter this passage is from Bachelard is generally arguing that good poetry involves novel creation (what he calls sublimation) — that in the poem there is something there which was not there before

even beyond the realm of poetry, sublimation can be seen as a form of free will

the psychoanalyst on the other hand analyzes the poem in terms of the poet's past... what happened to them as a child and how that caused them to write the poem etc.

Bachelard's latin quote here is pretty much just a zinger, he's saying if the psychoanalyst is interested in the original causes of things, they should just stay in the womb
